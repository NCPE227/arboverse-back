Why arbovirus matters?

Arboviruses (arthropod-borne viruses) are a growing global public health problem, causing a wide range of human diseases with high morbidity, mortality, and economic burdens.

Severe and deadly human diseases:
  • Encephalitis (e.g., West Nile Virus)
  • Arthritis (e.g., Chykungunya Virus)
  • Hemorrhagic fever (e.g., yellow fever)
Wide range of veterinary diseases:
  • Wildlife and domestic animals have disrupted ecosystems
  • Reduced livestock productivity
Billions of dollares in economic loses:
  • Overwhelming health systems
  • Increase inequalities globaly
Dengue fever disease
  • 10,000 deaths per year
  • 100 million cases per year
  • Over 125 countries affected
Yellow fever disease
  • 30,000 deaths per year
  • 200,000 cases per year
  • Forty-seven endemic countries in Africa and Cantral and South America
Bluetongue disease
  • ~US$ 3 billion in economic losses annually
  • Affects wild and domestic ruminants worldwide
