Why arbovirus matters?

Arboviruses (arthropod-borne viruses) are a growing global public health problem, causing a wide range of human and veterinary diseases with high morbidity, mortality, and economic burdens.

Severe and deadly human diseases:
  • Encephalitis
  • Arthritis
  • Hemorrhagic fever
Wide range of veterinary diseases:
  • Wildlife and domestic animals have disrupted ecosystems
  • Reduced livestock productivity
Billions of dollares in economic loses:
  • Overwhelming health systems
  • Increase inequalities globaly
Dengue virus
  • ~10,000 deaths annually
  • ~100 million cases annually
  • Over 125 countries and territories affected
Yellow fever virus
  • ~30,000 deaths annually
  • ~200,000 cases annually
  • Forty-seven endemic countries in Africa and Central and South America
Bluetongue virus
  • ~US$ 3 billion in economic losses annually
  • Affects wild and domestic ruminants worldwide
Zika virus
  • Microcephaly and other severe brain defects
  • Miscarriage, stillbirth, and other birth defects
  • 86 countries and territories affected
West Nile virus
  • Fatal neurological disease in humans
  • Cause severe disease and death in horses
  • US$ 56 million cost annually in the USA
African swine fever virus
  • Highly contagious for domestic and wild pigs
  • High mortality rates (95-100%)
  • US$111.2 billion economic loss in China in 2019
Chikungunya virus
  • Fever and acute and chronic polyarthralgia
  • Over 125 countries and territories affected
  • ~1.3 billion people live in areas at-risk for transmission
